## VID-20 · Campaign management walkthrough

**Maps to:** original task 20 · **Est:** 3h · **Status:** ⬜

### Goal — done means
A recording of the full campaign lifecycle from the operator's seat: create a campaign, configure AI
instructions, set calling rules, upload contacts, start and stop **safely**, check results.

### Why it matters more than it sounds
This is the **product**. Every other video explains how the machine works; this one shows what it is
for. It is also the video most likely to be watched by someone non-technical, so pitch it accordingly.

The word **safely** in the task is load-bearing: starting a campaign places real calls to real people.

### Plan
Use a **test campaign with a tiny contact list of numbers the user designates.** Never a real lead list.

1. **Create a campaign** — `campaigns/new`. Every field and what it affects downstream.
2. **AI instructions** — the AI Options surface: persona, company name, agent names, campaign slots,
   additional instructions, knowledge-driven mode. Explain the prompt architecture beneath it:
   a compliance floor plus per-model addenda, and why the compliance floor is not editable.
3. **LLM / voice selection** — the curated model menu and why others were excluded; TTS provider and
   voice. Mention that ~0.5 temperature is the working default and why. Note the Qwen 3.6 27B history:
   withdrawn once for evading the AI-disclosure question — a model that fails disclosure is a
   compliance problem, not a taste preference.
4. **Calling rules** — timezone, business hours, `ignore_schedule`, `batch_size`, `call_gap_seconds`,
   retry policy. **Explain the pacing arithmetic**: `batch_size: 1` with `call_gap_seconds: 300` is one
   call every five minutes, and a campaign configured that way looks broken while working exactly as
   configured. This is a real, recurring support question.
5. **Trunk and caller-ID** — how a campaign maps to a trunk, the per-campaign trunk snapshot, and the
   caller-ID verification requirement. Show the digit-count trap.
6. **Upload contacts** — CSV upload and paste-import, field mapping, validation, duplicate handling.
7. **Start / pause / stop / delete** — what each actually does. Note that delete is a **soft delete**
   (`status='deleted'`, filtered from listings) — nothing is destroyed.
8. **Watch it dial** — a small number of real calls to the designated test numbers. Show live status.
9. **Results** — the campaign detail view, calls, transcripts, summaries, recordings, dispositions.
10. **Safe stopping** — how to stop a campaign immediately and confirm no further calls are placed.

### Do
- Show the pacing arithmetic on screen. "Why isn't my campaign calling?" is most often a correctly
  configured campaign.
- Show the DNC path and say plainly that DNC is **never cached** — a number added to DNC is blocked on
  the very next attempt, deliberately, for compliance.
- Demonstrate stopping before demonstrating starting at scale. Teach the brake before the accelerator.

### Don't
- ❌ **Never start a campaign against a real lead list.** The allstate campaign holds ~691 real leads.
- ❌ Do not demonstrate deletion on a real campaign, even though it is a soft delete.
- ❌ Do not show real customer contact data during the upload section — use the test list.
- ❌ Do not skip the safety framing to make a smoother demo.

## VID-21 · Health and observability — what we actually have

**Maps to:** original task 21 (**retitled** — see revision R-8) · **Est:** 3h · **Status:** ⬜

### ⚠️ This task was rewritten, deliberately
Task 21 as written asks for *"how to read Grafana dashboards, what Prometheus alerts mean"*. There are
**three separate, non-unified** monitoring configuration sets in this repo — root +
`backend/deploy/prometheus/`, `infra/` (Kubernetes-oriented), and `telephony/observability/` — and
none has evidence of scraping production. `deploy/grafana/README.md` states outright that panels will
read "No data" until exporters exist. The root `alertmanager.yml` still contains unfilled `${...}`
placeholders for its Slack webhook. There is no redis-exporter on the box, which is why the dialer
liveness alert was delivered as a **systemd timer** rather than a Prometheus rule.

**Recording a walkthrough of dashboards that show no data would be actively misleading** — it would
leave viewers believing the system is monitored in a way it is not.

**What this video covers instead:** the monitoring that genuinely exists and genuinely works. The
Grafana/Prometheus gap is documented in DOC-09 and raised at REV-40 as a v2 decision.
**Building a monitoring stack is explicitly out of scope for this window.**

### Plan
1. **Open with the honest statement**: here is what we have, here is what we do not have, here is what
   we are doing about it. Thirty seconds, and it sets the frame for everything after.
2. **Health endpoints** — `/api/v1/healthz/deep` (dependency checks; the `000` pre-bind race after a
   restart) and `/api/v1/healthz/workers` (the three-worker registry, healthy/unhealthy semantics).
3. **The three liveness layers per worker** — Redis heartbeat key with TTL, systemd `Type=notify`
   watchdog (180s), and `Restart=always` with crash-loop guards. Explain the history: the dialer's
   heartbeat coroutine existed but was **never scheduled**, so a dead worker was invisible. Three
   layers exist because one was silently absent.
4. **`talky-healthwatch.timer`** — runs every 2 minutes, curls the worker probe, logs to journald.
   Show a real run. Note its limitation plainly: **it dies with the box it runs on**, which is why an
   external uptime monitor was added in TKT-013.
5. **journald triage** — the invocations that are actually useful, per unit, with worthwhile grep
   patterns. Cross-reference DOC-11.
6. **Sentry** — backend and frontend error tracking; how to read an issue, how release tagging works.
7. **`talky-trunk-status.timer`** — trunk registration status refreshed every 15 seconds into the DB.
8. **`talky-cleanup.timer`** — nightly at 03:00, currently `CLEANUP_DRY_RUN=true`, so it logs what it
   *would* delete. Show a dry-run log. State that flipping it to false is a deliberate decision
   pending retention sign-off.
9. **The gap, stated clearly** — no live metrics dashboard, no alerting pipeline beyond journald,
   Sentry and the external uptime check. What that means: slow degradation is not currently detected,
   only hard failure. That is the honest limitation and it is the most useful sentence in the video.

### Do
- Lead with the gap. A monitoring video that oversells coverage is worse than no video, because it
  produces false confidence.
- Show a real dry-run cleanup log. It is the clearest illustration of "configured but deliberately
  not enforced".
- Explain *why* three liveness layers exist. The story makes the design memorable.

### Don't
- ❌ Do not demonstrate Grafana dashboards populated with no data.
- ❌ Do not build monitoring to make the video better. Out of scope.
- ❌ Do not imply Prometheus alerting is active.
- ❌ Do not flip `CLEANUP_DRY_RUN` for a demonstration. It starts permanently deleting rows.
