### Plan
1. Compile the list from all six sources.
2. For each: what it is · why it was not done · **impact if it stays open** · agreed resolution.
3. Resolution is one of: **fix now** (today, if small) · **v2 backlog item** (with an owner) ·
   **accepted risk** (with a named person accepting it) · **needs a decision** (escalate today).
4. Sort by risk. Put the v2-blocking items at the top where they cannot be missed.
5. **Agree every resolution with the user before REV-42.** A gap list nobody has agreed to is a wish list.

- Distinguish "not done" from "deliberately not done". Both belong on the list; they are different rows.

  to prevent.
- ❌ Do not resolve a gap by silently reducing its scope.
- ❌ Do not leave anything as "we'll look at it later" with no owner and no date.

## REV-41 · Final architecture review

**Maps to:** original task 41 · **Est:** 2h · **Status:** ⬜ · **Depends on:** REV-40

### Goal — done means
A written confirmation that the system is in the intended state before v2: no known instabilities, no
undocumented workarounds, no unexplained degraded status. Where any of those exist, they are named and
owned rather than unknown.

### Plan
1. **Production health, live, today**: all units active · `/healthz/deep` 200 · `/healthz/workers` 3/3 ·
   `journalctl -p err` over the last 24h · a validation call.
2. **No unexplained degraded status.** Anything currently degraded must have a documented explanation.
   Note the known-and-correct case: the reminder worker does not retry Redis at boot and therefore
   reports unhealthy while DB work continues — right fail-direction, documented, not a mystery.
3. **No undocumented workarounds.** Walk DOC-09 and confirm every workaround in the system appears in
   it, with its reasoning. The test: could a new engineer encounter this and understand why it is
   there, from the docs alone?
4. **Known instabilities named.** Not zero — *named*. Zero would be a claim nobody can support. The
   review's job is that none is unknown.
5. **Architectural readiness for v2** — the honest assessment:
   - Single VPS, single API worker. **This is the ceiling** and v2 must address it.
   - No load-test data — the concurrency knee is unknown. That is the top measurement gap.
   - Layering is enforced (domain must not import from `app.api`, locked by an AST test) and the
     god files are split. Structurally, the codebase is in better shape than it was 30 days ago.
   - Multi-tenancy is enforced at the RLS layer with service-layer defence in depth and a test suite
     behind it.
6. Confirm the documentation set is canonical and the legacy sprawl is marked superseded.

### Do
- Say plainly what v2 must solve. That is the most useful output of this review.
- Base every claim on an artefact — a doc, a test result, a command output.

### Don't
- ❌ Do not claim "no known instabilities" if DOC-09 lists any. Name them instead.
- ❌ Do not sign off on architecture readiness while REV-40 gaps are unresolved and unowned.

### Checklist
- [ ] Live health verified today, output recorded
- [ ] Every degraded state explained
- [ ] Every workaround documented in DOC-09
- [ ] Known instabilities named
- [ ] Architectural ceiling stated for v2
- [ ] Documentation confirmed canonical; legacy marked superseded
- [ ] Written review completed

## REV-42 · V2 readiness sign-off

**Maps to:** original task 42 · **Est:** 1h · **Status:** ⬜ · **Depends on:** REV-39, REV-40, REV-41

### Goal — done means
Both parties sign, confirming all deliverables are complete and v2 development can begin.

### The sign-off document must state
1. **What was delivered** — 12 documents, 11 recordings, the code-stabilisation work, with links.
2. **What was not delivered, and why** — REV-40's gap list, with owners. Signing off with named gaps
   is normal and healthy. Signing off while pretending there are none is not.
3. **What changed from the original plan** — a pointer to `00-PLAN-REVISIONS.md`: nine assumptions in
   the original document did not survive contact with the codebase, and each was corrected with
   evidence rather than worked around.
4. **Production state** — commit hash, test-gate numbers, health status on the day.
5. **The conditions under which v2 begins** — what must be true, and what the v2 architecture work
   must address first (the single-worker ceiling and the absence of load data).
6. **Signatures** — both parties, dated.

### The honest question to ask before signing

> *Could an engineer who joined this week, given only `docs/v2/` and the recordings, understand what
> Talky.ai is, how it works, where everything lives, and how to deploy and debug it — without asking
> anyone?*

That is the goal the original document set. If the answer is no, sign-off is premature regardless of
how many boxes are ticked. If the answer is yes, the 30 days did what they were for.

### Do
- Test that question for real if you can. Give the docs to someone genuinely unfamiliar and see what
  they can and cannot answer. It is the only unbiased measure available.
- List the gaps in the sign-off itself, not in an appendix.

### Don't
- ❌ Do not sign with unowned gaps.
- ❌ Do not sign if REV-37/38's spot checks failed.
- ❌ Do not treat sign-off as a formality. It is the decision to build v2 on this foundation.

- [ ] Plan revisions referenced

- [ ] v2 starting conditions stated
- [ ] The "new engineer" question asked and answered honestly
